Key Features

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

The GPU is arguably the most critical component in a gaming PC as it is responsible for rendering graphics and images on the display. For gaming, a dedicated graphics card is essential, and high-end GPUs from companies like NVIDIA and AMD are commonly used.

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The CPU is the brain of the computer and plays a significant role in gaming, especially in tasks that require strong single-core performance. Many games rely heavily on a single core, so a high clock speed and strong single-core performance are essential for optimal gaming performance.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

RAM is crucial for storing and quickly accessing data that the CPU needs during gameplay. Games with high-resolution textures and complex environments benefit from larger RAM capacities.
